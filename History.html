Why we celebrate Republic day?
When India gained independence, it did not have its own permanent Constitution. Any nation without a strong Constitution is at risk of collapsing. To address this, a committee was formed under the leadership of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar,

which worked for almost three years to draft the Indian Constitution. On this day, 73 years ago, the Constitution of India came into force, officially making India a Republic. It is the longest written Constitution in the world, known for being both rigid yet flexible striking a perfect balance between federal and unitary features. Under British rule, Indians were denied even basic rights, and injustice was widespread. But on January 26, 1950, India finally gained the power to make its own laws. That is why Republic Day holds immense significance—it marks the day India became a sovereign, self-governing nation, free to uphold justice, equality, and democracy for all its citizens.
